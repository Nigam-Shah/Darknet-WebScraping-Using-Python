This is because the stomach is drastically slowing digestion, so the plant matter in your second dose of mushrooms will take much longer to digest, and therefore won’t be processed by your liver until much later. With pure psilocybin, you don’t have to wait on your silly ol’ stomach to digest everything.

║██ A note on how psilocybin works: ██║

Psilocybin is a stable molecule, although heat and UV radiation can cause it to break down (which is why it’s best to store this liquid in the refrigerator out of sunlight). However, the actual drug that makes your brain trip is called “psilocin.” Which means that your body needs to convert the psilocybin into psilocin before you feel the effects. There are two main ways your body breaks it down:

1. Strong acid (such as stomach acid)
2. Hepatic systems in the liver

If you take this liquid on an empty stomach, you will trip MUCH faster, since your stomach acid will break the psilocybin into psilocin right away. Expect to start feeling the effects in 5-10 minutes.

If your stomach is full, or you just don’t happen to have a lot of spare stomach acid at the moment, the trip will start once the psilocybin gets to your liver, gets converted to psilocin, then into your bloodstream, and past the blood-brain barrier where it starts dancing with your serotonin receptors. This process usually takes about an hour.

╔═══════════════════════╗
║██ How to avoid a “Bad Trip” ██║
╚═══════════════════════╝

These rules are the same for regular Shrooms as well as liquid Psilocybin

██ Rule #1: Don’t combine alcohol and mushrooms (unless you’re really experienced with mushrooms). The reason for this is two-fold: 1 - Alcohol is a depressant that acts on your entire central nervous system. Mushrooms slow down the part of your brain that normally acts as a filter for your conscious mind, so the two don’t compliment each other. Of course, mushrooms are so safe that you’re not hurting your body or brain by combining them, but alcohol makes it harder for you to control your thoughts and that can lead to mild psychological discomfort.

If you ever hear someone talk about their “bad trip,” ask them if they were drinking at the time. I’ll bet ya dollars to donuts that they had a few drinks before they started the trip.

██ Rule #2: Mindset. Whatever emotional/mental state you were in before you started tripping, you will probably continue on that path. But think of taking mushrooms this way: It’s like getting into a car that has the cruise control set at 40mph. You can steer the car wherever you want, but you can’t stop it or slow it down until it runs out of gas. So if you find yourself wanting the trip to stop because you’re feeling uncomfortable or worried about something, just remember that you can steer your mind somewhere else while you’re waiting for your body to finish metabolizing all of the psilocin - nothing can speed that process up. If you’re with someone and they’re having a bad trip, the easiest way to steer the mind somewhere else is to physically go to a different room. It works wonders. (keep in mind, if you are starting on the path to a ‘bad trip’ then you will NOT feel like moving. But walk into a different room anyway and start talking/thinking about something else. It’s magic.)

██ Rule #3: Setting. The location you choose for a hallucinogenic experience is very important. Your senses will be heightened (since the part of your brain that usually works to filter through your senses won’t be as active, so your conscious mind gets a LOT more input than it’s used to). So if you go someplace that is loud and has lots of lights/movement it can be overwhelming. The best place is somewhere you feel safe. If you’re going to trip in public, be with some friends you trust. If you’re going to trip in a nightclub for the first time, take some earplugs just in case the music gets too overwhelming. You can pop them in, sit down and close your eyes for a minute and feel fine again.

Again, If you’re with a friend and they start to have a ‘bad trip’ or start to feel just a bit uneasy – the easiest thing to do is to take them by the hand and move them to a different room. They might resist you, saying “No, I have to stay here.” Just be calm, assuring, and tell them that they can come back if they want to, but at least try to move. Two minutes after being in a new room (or hallway, or bathroom or whatever) they will feel differently.

██ Be on the super-safe side: Have some (0.5mg ~ 1.0mg) powdered Xanax (Alprazolam) handy. If someone is having a “Bad Trip” it’s due to anxiety, they can snort the powder and feel better in minutes. Xanax is an anti-anxiety drug; it will dull the trip, but at least it won’t be a unpleasant anymore.

═════════════════════════
Q: Should you take them on a full or empty stomach?
A: Either one is fine. On an empty stomach you will trip faster (since your stomach acid will be able to break down the stable psilocybin to the psychoactive psilocin faster). On an empty stomach, your trip can begin in less than 10 minutes (although allow another hour for your body to process the rest and start bringing on your peak).

On a full stomach, expect 45 to 90 minutes for the effects – depending on your metabolism and how much you ate. As a side note, know that psilocin (the psychoactive compound) is serotonergic, which means that our stomach (which has a LOT of serotonin receptors) will respond to it faster than our brains will. In effect, your stomach will start ‘tripping’ before your brain does. That means that when your trip starts, the stomach will slow digestion down tremendously – you will not be processing food much during your trip. This is also why your appetite goes away during a mushroom trip. Your stomach isn’t signaling hunger to your brain.
═════════════════════════

But since the amount of liquid in the vial is so small (8ml), you can dump it into something as small as a shot glass of orange juice, Gatorade, soda, or any other drink and it will almost completely mask the taste.

Q: How long does the liquid last before it loses potency?

A: Psilocybin is sensitive to heat and UV radiation. Normally the plant material itself shields the psilocybin from sunlight, but if you want to be extra safe, you can store it in the freezer. The vials I ship the liquid in are opaque to block some sunlight, but I wouldn’t leave them on the windowsill for weeks, or you will likely lose potency. I have personally had 1-year old liquid that I kept mostly in the refrigerator (It went with me to Burning Man and baked in the hot desert sun, then came back a week later unused) and it ended up being just as potent as ever.
